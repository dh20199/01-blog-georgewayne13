Pierre Trudeau

Pierre Trudeau was the 15th Prime Minister of Canada. He was born on October 18, 1919 in Quebec. He was educated in Quebec and earned his law degree from the University of Montreal and a Master’s in Political Science from Harvard University. He taught law at the University of Montreal for a number of years till he was elected to Parliament in 1965 where he rose quickly in Lester Pearson’s government becoming the Minister of Justice in 1967. Trudeau became the Leader of the Liberal Party of Canada in 1968 and lead them to a Majority Government in the 1968 election. He would continue to win further elections in 1972,, 1974, and 1980 only losing once in 1979. He had to deal with many challenging issue as Prime Minister of Canada such as the energy crisis , Quebec Separatism, and the partration of the Canadian Constitution. He passed away in 2000 at the age of 80, and was mourned across the nation. His actions as Prime Minister tended to create a polarizing reaction and has lead to constant debate about how good of a Prime Minister he was.
